IBD ontology for the domain of Inflammatory Bowel Disease, modelled from biomedical literature and clinical guidelines.

Create classes with the following hierarchy:

Root-level classes (parent_class Thing):
- InflammatoryBowelDisease: Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) refers to a group of lifelong diseases affecting the intestines.
- Symptom: IBD symptoms can range from mild to severe.
- DiseasePhase: Symptoms come and go in waves.
- Complication: IBD can lead to health complications both inside the GI tract and outside the GI tract.
- MentalHealthChallenge: IBD can impact a person's quality of life, increasing the risk of mental health challenges.
- Medication: Common medications for IBD.
- Treatment: Treatments for IBD.

Under InflammatoryBowelDisease, add two subclasses:
- UlcerativeColitis (Ulcerative Colitis): A main type of IBD.
- CrohnsDisease (Crohn's Disease): A main type of IBD.

Under Symptom (all are common symptoms of IBD):
- Diarrhea
- StomachPain (Stomach Pain)
- Fatigue
- Nausea
- WeightLoss (Weight Loss)

Under DiseasePhase:
- Remission: Periods when symptoms temporarily go away.
- FlareUp (Flare-up): Periods of increased symptoms.

Under Complication:
- GastrointestinalComplication (Gastrointestinal Complication): Complications inside the GI tract.
- ExtraintestinalManifestation (Extraintestinal Manifestation): Complications outside the GI tract.

Under GastrointestinalComplication:
- Dehydration
- Malabsorption: Not absorbing enough essential nutrients.
- IncreasedCancerRisk (Increased Cancer Risk): Increased risk of colon and rectal cancers.

Under ExtraintestinalManifestation:
- Anemia: Low red blood cell count.
- ReducedBoneDensity (Reduced Bone Density)
- JointPain (Joint Pain)
- SkinChanges (Skin Changes)
- EyeIrritation (Eye Irritation)
- DelayedGrowth (Delayed Growth): Delayed or impaired growth in some children.

Under MentalHealthChallenge:
- Depression: Mental health challenge associated with IBD.
- Anxiety: Mental health challenge associated with IBD.
- Distress: Feelings of distress or being overwhelmed.
- OtherMentalDisorder (Other Mental Disorder): Other mental disorders associated with IBD.

Under Medication:
- Aminosalicylate: These help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Also called 5-aminosalicylic acids or 5-ASA.
- Corticosteroid: Strong anti-inflammatory steroids that work fast.
- Immunomodulator: These block the immune reaction that leads to inflammation. Also called immunosuppressive agents.
- Biologic: These block the immune system chemicals that trigger inflammation. Made from living sources.

Under Treatment:
- Surgery: May be done to remove or widen the areas of the intestines that have been severely affected.

Object properties to create:
- affects: IBD affects the intestines. Domain: InflammatoryBowelDisease, Range: Thing.
- hasSymptom: IBD has symptoms. Domain: InflammatoryBowelDisease, Range: Symptom.
- hasPhase: IBD has phases of remission and flare-up. Domain: InflammatoryBowelDisease, Range: DiseasePhase.
- leadsTo: IBD can lead to complications. Domain: InflammatoryBowelDisease, Range: Complication.
- increasesRiskOf: IBD increases risk of mental health challenges. Domain: InflammatoryBowelDisease, Range: MentalHealthChallenge.
- treatedBy: IBD is treated by medications and surgery. Domain: InflammatoryBowelDisease, Range: Thing.
- isSymptomOf: is symptom of. Inverse of hasSymptom.
- isGIComplicationOf: is GI complication of.

- InflammatoryBowelDisease $treatedBy: Thing
